2. Look at the man page for ls. What does the -l flag do? What does the -a flag do?
	-l shows the long format details of what is in the directory
	-a shows all contents of the directory, including hidden files
	
3. How do you jump between words in the terminal?
	on my PC I can use Alt and the sideways arrows to jump between words
	
4. How do you get to the end of a line in terminal?
	On my PC Ctrl + e moves to the end of a line
	
5. How do you move your cursor to the beginning in terminal?
	On my PC Ctrl + a moves to the beginning of a line
	
6. How do you delete a word (without pressing backspace multiple times) in terminal?
	On my PC Ctrl + w deletes the word where the cursor is

	It is the path to a file fully written out from the root directory
	
9. What is an relative path?
	Relative path is the file path to the file from your current location

11. What do the r and f flags do with the rm command?
	 The r and f flags, when combined with the rm command allow the terminal to remove files and/or folders even if they are not empty, and does not require confirmation of the removal.
